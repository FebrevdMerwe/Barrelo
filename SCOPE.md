# Barrelo Scope Document

## Vision

Create a modern, extensible dart platform that separates dart detection from game logic, enabling an unlimited variety of games to be built on a single foundation.

The platform should not be designed as "another dart scoring application." Instead, it should become a platform for interactive dart experiences, allowing both traditional and completely new games to coexist.

---

# Objectives

The platform should:

* Detect every dart accurately and consistently.
* Support traditional dart games.
* Allow new games to be added without modifying the core platform.
* Provide an enjoyable experience for casual players and serious players alike.
* Support future expansion into online play, tournaments, leagues, training and AI-assisted experiences.

---

# Core Principles

## Modular

Games should exist independently from the platform.

Adding a new game should require minimal effort and should not impact existing games.

---

## Extensible

Every major feature should be designed with future growth in mind.

The platform should be able to expand into new game types, hardware, player experiences and integrations without requiring significant redesign.

---

## Maintainable

The platform should remain easy to understand and maintain as the number of games and features grows.

Clear separation of responsibilities should minimise the impact of future changes.

---

## Reliable

The platform should provide a dependable experience suitable for both home and commercial environments.

Players should have confidence that dart detection and game progression are consistent.

---

## User Friendly

The platform should be intuitive for players of all experience levels.

Starting a game should require minimal setup and navigation.

---

## Fast

Game flow should feel immediate.

Players should not experience noticeable delays between throwing a dart and receiving game feedback.

---

# Target Users

The platform should support:

* Casual home players
* Families
* Competitive players
* Clubs
* Bars and entertainment venues
* Tournament organisers
* Coaches
* Developers creating new games

---

# Functional Scope

## Player Management

Support individual players and groups.

Players should be able to build profiles and retain historical information.

---

## Games

The platform should include traditional dart games while allowing unlimited custom games to be added over time.

Each game should operate independently without affecting others.

---

## Statistics

Provide meaningful statistics for players.

Statistics should be available globally and within individual games where appropriate.

---

## Training

Support practice modes focused on improving specific skills.

Training experiences should be expandable over time.

---

## Multiplayer

Support local multiplayer initially, with future support for online multiplayer.

---

## Competition

Allow organised competitions including leagues and tournaments as future enhancements.

---

## Spectator Experience

Provide an engaging experience for people watching games through clear visual feedback and game progression.

---

# Non-Functional Goals

The platform should be:

* Responsive
* Stable
* Scalable
* Easy to learn
* Easy to maintain
* Easy to extend
* Consistent across all games

# Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful when it:

* Provides reliable dart detection.
* Makes adding new games straightforward.
* Supports both classic and innovative gameplay.
* Delivers a smooth player experience.
* Can continue growing for many years without requiring architectural redesign.
* Encourages a community to create and share new games.
* Serves as a platform rather than a single-purpose scoring application.
